Protecting the Pace: F1's Commitment to Safety and Well-being

F1's safety innovations protect lives on and off the track. We also promote fan and driver physical and mental well-being.

Timeline of Safety Tech: Protecting Lives

Explore the key innovations, from the introduction of the HANS device to the implementation of the Halo, that have saved countless lives and made F1 the safest it has ever been.

1996

Tethered Wheels

Prevents wheels from flying off in accidents, protecting drivers and spectators.

2003

HANS Device (Head and Neck Support)

Reduces the risk of basilar skull fractures in high-deceleration incidents.

2018

The Halo

A mandatory cockpit protection system significantly reducing head injuries from flying debris or impacts.
